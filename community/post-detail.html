Low-GI Ragi Dosa — My Sunday Morning Staple

Priya Sharma
Type 2 diabetic for 4 years · Bangalore
12 Mar 2026
4 min read

I've been Type 2 for four years now. For the first two, I basically gave up on South Indian breakfast because I assumed dosa was off the table. Then I started experimenting with the batter, and the results genuinely surprised me.

Why Ragi?

Ragi (finger millet) has a glycemic index of around 68 compared to regular white rice at 73+. More importantly, it's high in fibre, which slows glucose absorption. Combined with oats, I got a batter that holds together beautifully and keeps me full well past lunch.

The Batter (makes 8–10 dosas)

  • 1 cup ragi flour
  • ½ cup rolled oats (blended to a coarse flour)
  • ¼ cup urad dal (soaked 4 hours, ground smooth)
  • Salt to taste, water to consistency
  • Ferment overnight — the sourness actually helps further lower GI

My Numbers Before and After

Before switching: my 2-hour post-breakfast reading was typically 178–195 mg/dL. After three weeks of this batter (same quantity, same meal time): 138–152 mg/dL. I'm not saying this will work the same for everyone, but it's been consistent enough for me to call it my staple.

Tips That Make a Difference

  • Eat with a protein-first — I always have a small bowl of curd before the dosa
  • Don't skip the coconut chutney — the fat actually helps slow absorption
  • Walk for 10 minutes after, even just around the house
  • Check your reading 2 hours post-meal at least the first few times so you know how your body responds

I hope this helps someone who's given up on breakfast the way I did. Happy to answer questions in the comments.
