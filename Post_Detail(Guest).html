Cauliflower rice completely changed my dinner routine

Sarah J. Type 2 · 4 yrs
Posted October 12, 2024 · 3 min read

I was skeptical at first. Everyone kept recommending cauliflower rice but I was convinced it would taste like watery mush. Then my sugar readings kept spiking after dinner and I decided to actually try it properly.

The key mistake most people make is steaming it. You have to dry fry it in a pan first — no oil, just heat. Let it sit on medium heat for about 8 minutes until some of the moisture cooks off. Then add your oil and season it like normal fried rice.

After switching, my post-dinner readings dropped noticeably — not dramatically, but consistently. I've been doing this for 3 months now and I genuinely enjoy it. It goes well with curries, stir-fries, and dal. I haven't missed regular rice nearly as much as I expected.

If you're new to this, start by mixing half cauliflower half regular rice. The difference in taste is subtle enough that you'll barely notice, but the glycaemic impact is quite different.
